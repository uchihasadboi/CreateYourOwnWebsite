Rules

There are many ways to complete a Nuzlocke challenge, but below are the rules that must be followed to consider it one.

  •   You may only catch the first Pokemon you encounter on each route
  •   If any of your Pokemon faint, you must consider them "dead" and either release them, or place them in the PC. They cannot be used again.
  •   You must nickname all of the Pokemon you catch.

In addition to the previous rules, many people have found that adding further stipulations can increase the difficulty. The first three rules below are typically referred to as "Hardcore" mode. These rules are optional, but can elevate the experience and are recommended for people that are looking for a challenge. The following two rules are stipulations that most people add to their casual and hardcore Nuzlockes so they have more diverse options, and can use shiny Pokemon if they happen to be so lucky.

  •   No items in battle, except for held items and Pokeballs for wild encounters.  *Hardcore*
  •   None of your Pokemon can be a higher level than the gym leaders highest level Pokemon. No over-leveling!  *Hardcore*
  •   If you completely white out to a trainer, you must start over your run.  *Hardcore*
  •  
  •   If you encounter a Pokemon from an evolutionary line that you have already caught, then you can re-roll your encounter. This is commonly known as the duplicate clause.
  •   If you encounter a shiny Pokemon, you can catch it and replace it with the non-shiny version you already own. If you do not own that shiny, you can catch it and hope you find the matching encounter later in the game. This is referred to as the shiny clause

The most important thing to consider when you are attempting a Nuzlocke is that none of these rules actual matter! This is meant to be a fun way to play a nostalgic childrens game that adds a level of risk, while being rewarding when you find yourself out of a tough spot. If you are not having fun with your current rule set, change it. You're not cheating if you're the one making the rules!

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the purpose of a Nuzlocke?

  •   There really isn't one. These are self imposed rules which are intended to increase the difficulty of Pokemon games, while making it much more strategic. While doing a Nuzlocke you have to understand when and how to use moves effectively, when to swap your Pokemon out when they're in danger of dying, how to use held items efficiently, how to team build, and how to maintain momentum in battles. You need a fair amount of knowledge about Pokemon, such as: their types/strengths/weaknesses, their movesets, when they evolve/if they evolve, their abilities, their stats, and most important, how well they fit in your current team. The resources on the Helpful Tools page can be a great starting point for anyone interested in attempting their first Nuzlocke!

How do you win in a Nuzlocke challenge?

  •   The goal of a Nuzlocke is to defeat all 8 Gym Leaders(or 16 if you're in Johto), and then defeat the Elite Four and Champion. This can be done as you like, but you must at least follow the first three rules posted above for it to be considered a "true" Nuzlocke .

Where does the name come from?

  •   The creator of the original Nuzlocke created a webcomic back in 2010 showcasing the new rules he had implemented into his challenge run of Pokemon Ruby and posted it on 4chan, where it was discovered and adopted by many people on the site. The original webcomic had a Nuzleaf that was a reference to John Locke, who is a character in the TV show Lost known for doing things in a hard way, and thus the Nuz-Locke was born. The ruleset found its way to the internet and the rest is gaming history.

What happens if you are doing a hardcore run and your Pokemon over-level during a Gym Leader battle?

  •   The general rule of thumb across the community is that the level cap stops as soon as you start the battle with a Gym Leader. A lot of people will actually train all of their Pokemon up until they are about to level up over the level cap, thus giving them a level advantage as soon as they can KO an opponent Pokemon.

What Pokemon game would be best for a beginner Nuzlocker?

  •   This has been a topic of great discussion, but the community believes the easiest game to Nuzlocke would be FireRed or LeafGreen. These games are based on the original two games, Red and Blue, but with updated Generation Three mechanics. The gameplay is somewhat slower paced than future generations, and allows a great introduction to managing your Pokemon effectively, while soaking up all that nostalgic charm of the First Generation.

What happens if all the Pokemon you have over-level before you reach the next Gym Leader? Are you allowed to catch more?

  •   Good luck on your next run! Jokes aside, this would actually be a restart as you are out of usable Pokemon. As I stated before, at the end of the day, these runs are meant to be fun. So, play however you wish!

Does it matter if I play in switch mode versus set mode?

  •   This is a great question. The difference between Switch and Set is essentially the difference between easy and hard. Switch Mode allows you to be aware of the next Pokemon your opponent is going to send out, and gives you an opportunity to switch out your current Pokemon for one that has a better matchup. This somewhat trivializes the purpose of a Nuzlocke, as you can almost always have a solution to what could otherwise be a problem. Playing in Set Mode forces you to play intelligently and make defensive decisions, knowing that whatever Pokemon you might want to send in is going to take damage from your opponents Pokemon. Although I did not state this rule earlier, it does play an important part in the makeup of a Nuzlocke. Set Mode is the preferred rulestyle, but again, you can play however you wish!

I don't like grinding for levels on my Pokemon, is it okay to cheat in Rare Candies?

  •    Absolutely, this is actualy highly encouraged. The whole point of a Nuzlocke is to have fun and test yourself, not to sit there and mindlessly press the A button for 45 minutes. If you are playing on a *Completely Legit, Totally Not an Emulator, Nintendo DS*, you can find Gameshark Codes online that can assist you in this.
